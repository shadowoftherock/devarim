## ‚úÖ 1. **Cohabitation/Live-in Partnership Agreement (Contract)**

Under Philippine law, unmarried couples (opposite-sex or same-sex) may enter into a **private contract** spelling out their rights and obligations as partners ‚Äî often called a *cohabitation agreement* or *domestic partnership declaration*. ([Respicio & Co.][2])

* Terms for separation (if you choose one)
* Support obligations (where lawful)

**Legal basis:**

* The *Civil Code* allows parties to contract freely as long as the agreement:

  * Has mutual consent,
  * Deals with lawful objectives,
  * Has a valid cause,
  * Does not violate public policy or morals. ([Respicio & Co.][2])

**Important details:**

* **It doesn‚Äôt create a marriage-equivalent status.** Philippine law does *not* recognize ‚Äúcommon-law marriage‚Äù or domestic partnership as a legal family status the same way marriage is recognized. ([LawShun][1])

Because Philippine law sees **marriage as a unique legal institution**, any personal ‚Äúvow‚Äù or contract will not:

# B. What if the couple decides to separate?

A **Cohabitation or Live-in Partnership Agreement** can generally be **terminated or ‚Äúbroken‚Äù if the couple decides to separate**, but there are some important nuances:

---

### 1. **Nature of the Agreement**

* This agreement is a **private contract** between partners outlining rights, obligations, and arrangements regarding finances, property, and other matters during the relationship.
* It is **not the same as marriage**, so it usually doesn‚Äôt involve divorce law, but contract law applies.

---

### 2. **Termination Conditions**

* Most agreements include a **‚Äútermination clause‚Äù** specifying how it ends‚Äîoften when:

  * Partners separate or move apart permanently.
  * Certain conditions are met (like sale of jointly owned property).
* If the agreement is **silent on termination**, general contract principles apply: mutual consent can end it.

---

### 3. **Obligations After Separation**

* Even after ending the agreement, any obligations outlined in it may remain enforceable. Examples:

  * Division of jointly owned assets.
  * Payment of shared debts.
  * Support or reimbursement agreed upon in the contract.

---

### 4. **Legal Considerations**

* In some countries (like the Philippines), cohabitation agreements are **recognized under civil contract law**, but enforcement may vary.
* Any attempt to break the agreement **unilaterally without consent** could lead to a dispute in court.
* It‚Äôs often recommended to **put the termination in writing** and have both parties sign it.

---

‚úÖ **Summary:**
Yes, a live-in partnership agreement can end if the couple separates, **especially if both agree**, but you need to handle it formally and honor any remaining obligations.
